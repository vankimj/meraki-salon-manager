# Post-Demo Follow-up Email Templates

**Purpose:** Three template variants based on how the demo went. Send within 24 hours (ideally same day, evening). Always personalized — never paste the template verbatim.

**Universal rules:**
1. **Specific.** Reference at least 2 things they said. Generic emails get archived.
2. **Honest.** If they're a bad fit, say so kindly — don't pretend.
3. **Easy.** One clear next action. No "let me know your thoughts!" — that's a closing nobody acts on.
4. **Timing.** Same day if possible, max 24 hours. Anything later loses the demo's emotional residue.

---

## Variant A — Excited prospect (high signal: asked migration questions, liked specific features)

**Use when:** They leaned in, asked about migration, asked about specific features beyond the basics, asked when they could start.

```
Subject: Migration plan for [Their Salon Name]

Hi [first name],

Really enjoyed the call. A few specifics from our conversation:

- You mentioned [specific pain they raised — e.g. "losing track of which client wants
  text vs email vs phone calls"]. The Comms Hub fixes exactly that, and it's in
  the Comms Pack ($19/mo on top of any plan).

- You're currently on [their platform]. Migration takes one business day end-to-end.
  Here's what we'd do for [Their Salon Name] specifically:
  · Day 1 morning: I export your client + transaction history from [their platform]
    and run the import on a sandbox. You verify the dedupe is right.
  · Day 1 afternoon: We import your service menu, set up your staff, configure your
    online booking page. You're live by close of business.
  · First week: I'm on call (literally) for any front-desk question.

- Your team size puts you on Studio ($49/mo). With Comms Pack + AI Pack you mentioned
  wanting, you're at $49+$19+$19 = $87/mo total. (You're paying [estimated current
  cost] today across [their tools].)

Three options for next steps:
  1. Apply for Founders' Cohort: [link to intake form]
     — Free Solo for life, but you'd want Studio for your team size, so this is more
       about being part of the cohort than the discount.
  2. Just sign up for Studio + your packs: I'll send the link with your migration
     window pre-scheduled.
  3. Sit on it for a week: totally fine, no pressure. Reply when you're ready.

Pick whichever feels right. I'm not going anywhere.

— Jonathan
```

## Variant B — Hesitant prospect (mid signal: interested but raised concerns)

**Use when:** They liked the demo but raised real objections — price, timing, team buy-in, switching cost. Don't push past their hesitation; address it directly.

```
Subject: Following up — and the things you mentioned wanting to think about

Hi [first name],

Thanks for the time today. Wanted to follow up on the things you said you'd
need to think about:

[For each concern they raised, address it specifically. Examples:]

> "I'd want my front desk to weigh in"
- Totally reasonable. I'd love to do a 15-min demo with [front desk lead's name]
  on a separate call. No need for you to be on it — they'll have different
  questions than you did.

> "Switching mid-busy-season feels risky"
- Agreed. We can schedule the migration for [slow window — e.g. "the week after
  Mother's Day rush" / "January"]. Your account is provisioned the day you sign
  up; we just delay the data import + cutover.

> "Pricing feels like a lot for what we use"
- Also fair. Looking at what you described, you might not need [specific pack]
  right away. Solo is free during Founders' Year if you want to see the platform
  in action with your real data before committing to a paid plan. Then add packs
  as you actually use the features.

No pressure either way. If switching isn't right for you right now, that's a
fine answer too — and I'd rather hear that than keep following up.

If anything changes or you have follow-up questions for me or your team has
questions for me, I'm here.

— Jonathan
```

## Variant C — Not a fit (low signal: wrong size, wrong stage, wrong industry)

**Use when:** Demo revealed they're outside ICP (50+ staff, multi-state franchise, or a single-staff stylist who really just wants Square's free booking).

Two reasons to send a real email here instead of ghosting:
1. They might refer the right kind of customer
2. Your reputation in the salon community matters

```
Subject: Honest take after our call

Hi [first name],

Thanks for the call. I want to be straight with you — I don't think Plume
Nexus is the right fit for [Their Salon Name] right now, and I want to be
upfront about why rather than waste your time with follow-ups.

You're [too large for our current product / too early in your business / using
your current setup well already / etc.]. I'd love to revisit when [specific
condition that would change the fit, e.g. "you've grown to 5+ staff" or "your
current platform stops meeting your needs"], but pushing you to switch today
wouldn't serve you.

A few things you might find useful in the meantime:
- [Specific recommendation: a free tool, an article, an alternative platform
  honestly suited to them]

If you know other salon owners in the [3-15 staff] range who might be a better
fit — I'd love an intro. And feel free to send anyone with questions to
hello@plumenexus.com.

Appreciate you spending time with me.

— Jonathan
```

## Variant D — They no-showed

**Use when:** They booked but didn't show up.

```
Subject: Looks like we missed each other today

Hi [first name],

Looks like our call didn't connect today — no worries, life happens. If you
still want to chat, here's [Calendly link] to grab another time. No pressure
either way.

If you've decided it's not the right time, just reply with "not now" and I'll
take you off my follow-up list — no offense taken.

— Jonathan
```

(Send this once. If they don't reply, drop them. Don't chase.)

---

## Variant E — They went silent after demo

**Use when:** Demo went well, you sent Variant A or B, two weeks have passed, no response.

```
Subject: Closing the loop

Hi [first name],

Following up one more time on Plume Nexus — it's been a couple weeks since we
chatted and I haven't heard back. I want to respect your time and inbox, so I'll
stop pinging after this one.

If you'd like to:
- **Move forward:** [Calendly link]
- **Need more time:** reply with a date you'd want me to circle back
- **Pass for now:** just reply "pass" — I'll close the file

Either way, no judgment. Wishing you a good [season — e.g. "holiday rush"
or "summer slowdown"].

— Jonathan
```

After this one, stop. If they want to come back, they will.
